# Local Services Ads (LSA / "Google Guaranteed") — Launch Playbook

The best paid channel for home services: **pay per lead** (not per click), a green
**Google Guaranteed** badge, and placement **above** both the map pack and regular ads.
Set it up at **google.com/localservices** (sign in with the SAME Google account as your GBP).

> Reality check: LSA is run inside Google's Local Services platform, not the website.
> Leads arrive as calls/messages in the **Local Services Ads app** on your phone.

---

## What LSA covers for Rally
- **Primary category: Pressure washing** — covers house washing, roof washing, concrete
  cleaning, soft washing, etc. This is your summer demand engine.
- **Lighting (permanent + Christmas) is NOT an LSA category.** Plan **Search Ads** for
  those, timed for late summer/fall when people search (see Phase 2). Don't wait on LSA
  to advertise lighting.

## Ranking factors (what wins you more leads)
1. **Reviews** — count, rating, and recency. (Jobber is already feeding these — keep it on.)
2. **Responsiveness** — answer LSA calls/messages FAST. Google demotes slow responders.
3. **Proximity** — to the searcher; your service-area cities matter.
4. **Hours** — you only get leads during open hours. Keep them accurate and wide.
5. **Budget** — higher weekly budget = eligible for more leads (but you only pay per lead).

## Step-by-step setup
1. Go to **google.com/localservices** → "Get started." Sign in with your GBP Google account.
2. Confirm **category = Pressure washing** and your **service-area cities** (use your 12:
   Wheeling, St. Clairsville, Bridgeport, Martins Ferry, Bellaire, Moundsville, Triadelphia,
   Wellsburg, Bethlehem, Powhatan Point, Barnesville, Cadiz).
3. Add the **services you offer** (house washing, roof washing, concrete cleaning, etc.).
4. Upload **insurance** (and **license** if requested).
5. Complete the **background check** — Google's vendor emails you a link (owner + field staff).
6. **Connect your Google Business Profile** so reviews + business info flow in.
7. Set your **weekly budget** (see below).
8. **Go live** once you see the **Google Guaranteed** badge (verification can take a few days).

## Budget — how it works + where to start
- You set a **weekly budget** = the max you want to spend. Google charges **per valid lead**
  (a call or message from a real prospect). Daily spend fluctuates; weekly stays near your cap.
- **Per-lead cost varies by market** — Google shows your local estimate during setup. In a
  mid-size market like the Ohio Valley, exterior-cleaning leads are typically modest. Trust
  the estimate Google shows you over any number online.
- **Start by capacity, not vanity.** Ask: *how many estimate calls can I actually handle and
  close per week?* Set the budget to roughly that many leads. A lean start lets you measure
  before scaling.
- **The math that matters** (fill in your real numbers):
  - Cost per lead × leads = spend
  - Leads × your close rate = booked jobs
  - Booked jobs × average job value = revenue
  - Example: $25/lead, 10 leads = $250. Close 1 in 3 → ~3 jobs. At ~$400/job → ~$1,200 revenue
    on $250 spend. **When the math works like that, scale the budget up.**
- **Recommended first move:** start small (enough for a handful of leads/week), watch close
  rate and revenue for 2–3 weeks, then increase budget on what's profitable.

## Don't pay for junk — dispute bad leads
You can **dispute** leads that are spam, the wrong service, or out of your area, and Google
**credits** them. Do it promptly in the app — it keeps your true cost-per-real-lead low.

## Daily habits that lower your cost
- **Answer fast** (use the Local Services app). Missed calls hurt ranking and waste budget.
- **Mark lead outcomes** (booked / not a fit) — it trains the algorithm to send better leads.
- **Keep reviews flowing** (Jobber) and **reply to them** in GBP.
